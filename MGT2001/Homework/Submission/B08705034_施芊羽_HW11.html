11.5¶

  • $H_0$: The plane is on fire.
  • $H_1$: The plane is not on fire. ##### Type of errors:
  • Type I: The plane is on fire but the pilot chose not to land immediately:
    • Result: Several death and injuries.
  • Type II: The plane is not on fire but the pilot chose to land immediately:
    • Result: The waste of money and time of the suddenly landing as well as the damage to the plane caused by immediately-landing.

If I'm the pilot of the plane, I'll choose to land immediately. Since the death and injuries are more difficult to recover and make up than the money and time wasted due to pre-landing, the decision to land immediately will be better than continuing flying.

h0_mean = 1000
n = 25
std = 200
alpha = 0.05
zcv = stats.norm.ppf(1 - alpha / 2)
ucl = h0_mean + zcv * std / (n ** 0.5)
lcl = h0_mean - zcv * std / (n ** 0.5)
mean_vec = np.arange(800, 1200, 0.1)
beta_vec = np.zeros(mean_vec.shape[0])
for i, h1_mean in enumerate(mean_vec):
    z = (lcl - h1_mean) / (std / (n ** 0.5))
    p = -stats.norm.cdf(z)
    z = (ucl - h1_mean) / (std / (n ** 0.5))
    p = p + stats.norm.cdf(z)
    beta_vec[i] = p

_ = plt.plot(mean_vec, beta_vec)
